# Project-Price Video Content Brief

This brief defines the required message direction for AI-generated social videos.

## Core audience and emotion

- Audience: realtors advising potential home buyers during showings and offer decisions.
- Secondary audience: buyers worried about hidden repair costs after inspection.
- Emotional state: urgency, uncertainty, and budget pressure before making an offer.
- Outcome: fast confidence from immediate Project Price estimate ranges and clear repair priorities.

## Required story themes

- Realtor spots a potential repair issue while touring a home with a buyer.
- Realtor uses Project Price on the spot to generate immediate estimate ranges.
- Buyer reviews Basic vs Standard vs Premium scope and decides negotiation strategy.
- Realtor explains how repair ranges affect offer price, credits, or seller concessions.
- Buyer avoids surprise costs by seeing realistic local-market ranges before closing.

## Creative pivot options

Each video should pick one primary creative pivot so the series does not feel repetitive.

- Pivot A: "Inspection surprise" (unexpected repair risk discovered mid-tour).
- Pivot B: "Offer strategy" (how estimate ranges change bid amount and terms).
- Pivot C: "Credit negotiation" (buyer requests seller credits using estimate evidence).
- Pivot D: "Deal saver" (realtor keeps deal alive by reframing scope and phases).
- Pivot E: "Budget reality check" (buyer compares dream upgrades vs must-fix items).
- Pivot F: "Multiple bids confusion" (Project Price clarifies spread between quotes).
- Pivot G: "Time-pressure decision" (same-day offer deadline with quick estimate support).
- Pivot H: "First-time buyer coaching" (plain-language education through the estimate tiers).

## Creative variation controls

Rotate these controls between videos to create fresh outputs while preserving the same core message.

- Perspective: buyer POV, realtor POV, split POV, or narrated case-study recap.
- Property type: starter home, older fixer-upper, flipped home, move-in-ready with hidden risks.
- Repair category focus: roof, foundation, plumbing, electrical, HVAC, kitchen, bath, exterior envelope.
- Stakes: low-risk cosmetic, moderate functional, high-risk safety/code urgency.
- Negotiation outcome: price reduction, repair credit, seller-completed repairs, phased post-close plan.
- Emotional tone: anxious, skeptical, relieved, confident, strategic.
- Pace format: 15-second quick hook, 30-second walkthrough, 45-second mini case study.

## Episode formula (recommended)

- Hook: show a visible concern and buyer uncertainty.
- Discovery: realtor identifies likely issue and opens Project Price.
- Estimate: show Basic, Standard, Premium ranges with one-line scope differences.
- Decision: buyer and realtor choose negotiation path.
- Outcome: clear next step (offer revision, credit request, contractor verification).

## Rotation strategy (recommended)

Do not force every concept into one 8-second video.

- Treat each short as one micro-story with one clear job.
- Rotate reference angles across a sequence so the full value proposition is covered over time.
- Keep each clip focused on one repair category and one negotiation outcome.

Use this simple playlist model:

- Clip 1: Identify problem risk.
- Clip 2: Show immediate estimate range.
- Clip 3: Explain Basic vs Standard vs Premium scope shift.
- Clip 4: Apply offer strategy (price adjustment).
- Clip 5: Apply credit strategy (seller concession).
- Clip 6: Show buyer confidence outcome and next step.

Then repeat with a new property type, new repair category, and new emotional tone.

## Visual scene guidance

- Residential construction and renovation context only.
- Show realtor + buyer walkthrough moments in realistic for-sale homes.
- Show in-app estimate review moments: phone in hand, line-item discussion, budget decisions.
- Show pricing conversation moments: repair credits, concession requests, offer adjustments.
- Keep visuals practical and believable for real homeowner jobs.

## Tone and message rules

- Tone: practical, trustworthy, advisor-led, non-hype.
- Focus on education and transparency, not pressure or fear marketing.
- Avoid promising exact final prices before on-site contractor validation.
- Explain that estimates are immediate ranges tied to scope and local market factors.
- Position Project Price as a decision-support tool for offer strategy, not a final bid contract.

## Do not use

- Multifamily property-operations messaging.
- Commercial maintenance framing.
- Generic plumbing-only narratives unless tied to buyer repair decisions.
- Unrealistic bargain claims or guaranteed lowest-price statements.

## Prompt anchor (drop-in)

Use this as the base instruction for video-generation prompts:

"Create a realistic short social video where a realtor advises a potential buyer about likely home repairs and uses Project Price to provide an immediate estimate range. Show the buyer and realtor discussing Basic, Standard, and Premium repair options, then using those ranges to shape offer strategy, repair credits, or concessions. Keep the message focused on estimate transparency, scope-driven pricing, and confident decision-making in a residential home-buying context."
