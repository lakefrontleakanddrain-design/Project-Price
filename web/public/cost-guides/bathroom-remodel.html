Frequently Asked Questions

What is the ROI on a bathroom remodel?

Mid-range bathroom remodels recoup roughly 60–70% of cost at resale. An upscale primary bath addition can return 50–60%. Cosmetic updates (new vanity, fixtures, paint) typically offer the best value-per-dollar because they refresh the look at a fraction of a full gut cost.

Do I need a permit for a bathroom remodel?

Permits are required if you're moving plumbing, adding electrical circuits, or altering structural walls. Replacing fixtures in place (same location) typically does not require a permit, but this varies by municipality. Your contractor should verify local requirements.

How do I save money on a bathroom remodel?

Keep fixtures in the same location (moving plumbing is expensive), choose a prefab shower surround instead of custom tile, use large-format tiles (fewer grout lines = less labor), and shop clearance sections at tile distributors.
