Frequently Asked Questions

What is the cheapest fence to install?

Chain link is the cheapest at $15–$30/linear ft installed. For privacy at low cost, pressure-treated wood dog-ear fence runs $20–$35/ft and is the most common budget option across the US.

Is vinyl or wood fence cheaper?

Wood is cheaper upfront ($20–$45/ft vs $25–$55/ft for vinyl). Over 15–20 years, vinyl often costs less when you factor in wood's maintenance (staining, sealing, board replacement). Vinyl won't rot, warp, or splinter.

How long does fence installation take?

A 150-linear-foot fence takes 1–3 days for a professional crew. Post setting requires concrete curing time (24–48 hours) before panels are attached. Complex terrain, gates, and corners add time.

Do I need to call 811 before digging fence posts?

Yes — always. Call 811 (the national "Call Before You Dig" service) at least 3 business days before digging. This is legally required in most states and is free. Underground utility lines will be marked at no cost.
