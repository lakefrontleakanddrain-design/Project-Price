What Affects Painting Cost

Prep work

Scraping peeling paint, patching holes, sanding, and priming can double labor time. Older homes with lead paint require special handling under EPA RRP rules, adding $500–$2,000+ in compliance costs.

Number of colors and coats

Going from a dark to light color (or vice versa) typically requires an extra coat, adding 20–30% to material cost. Accent walls and trim in different colors add labor time.

Ceiling height

Standard 8–9 ft ceilings are included in room pricing. Vaulted or cathedral ceilings (12+ ft) add $150–$400 per room due to specialized equipment and extra safety setup.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to paint a room?

A professional painter charges $300–$900 for a standard bedroom (roughly 400 sq ft of wall surface). This includes labor and materials for two coats of paint.

How long does interior painting take?

A single room takes 4–8 hours. A whole-home interior (4–5 bedrooms, living areas) takes 3–5 days for a professional crew.

How often should I repaint the exterior?

Every 7–10 years for most siding types. Wood siding needs attention every 5–7 years. Signs it's time: fading, peeling, cracking, or chalking when you run your hand across the surface.
