Frequently Asked Questions

What costs the most in a kitchen remodel?

Cabinets represent 30–40% of total kitchen remodel cost. In a $40,000 project, expect $12,000–$16,000 for cabinetry alone. The next biggest cost centers are appliances (15–20%), countertops (10–15%), and labor markup from a general contractor if used.

How long does a kitchen remodel take?

Minor updates take 1–2 weeks. A mid-range remodel runs 3–6 weeks. Full gut remodels with layout changes typically take 8–16 weeks including permit wait times.

Do I need a permit for a kitchen remodel?

Permits are typically required if you're moving plumbing, changing electrical service, or altering structural walls. Cosmetic updates (paint, countertops, cabinet refacing) generally do not require permits. Your contractor should handle permit applications.

What's the ROI on a kitchen remodel?

Mid-range kitchen remodels recoup roughly 60–75% of cost at resale according to Remodeling Magazine's Cost vs. Value report. A $40,000 remodel might add $25,000–$30,000 in home value. High-end remodels in ordinary neighborhoods often recoup less.
