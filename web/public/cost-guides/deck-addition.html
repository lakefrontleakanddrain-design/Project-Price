Frequently Asked Questions

Is composite decking worth the extra cost?

Over 15–20 years, composite decks often cost less than wood when you factor in staining, sealing, and board replacement. They also hold color better and resist rot, mold, and insects. If you plan to stay in your home long-term, composite is typically the smarter investment.

Do I need a permit to build a deck?

Yes in most jurisdictions, especially for attached decks or those elevated more than 18 inches. Permits typically cost $200–$600. Always verify with your local building department — your contractor should handle the application.

What is the ROI on adding a deck?

Decks typically return 60–75% of their cost at resale. A $15,000 pressure-treated deck may add $9,000–$11,000 in home value. Composite decks return slightly less in dollar terms but may be more attractive to buyers.
