Additional Cost Factors

Old floor removal

Removing and disposing of old flooring adds $1–$3 per sq ft. Tile removal is the most labor-intensive at $2–$4 per sq ft due to adhesive and subfloor patching.

Subfloor repair

Damaged or uneven subfloor must be corrected before new flooring is installed. Repairs run $500–$3,000 depending on extent. Self-leveling compound adds $2–$5 per sq ft.

Stairs

Flooring stairs adds $75–$150 per step for hardwood or LVP. A standard 14-step staircase adds $1,050–$2,100 to a project.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the most durable flooring?

Porcelain tile is the most durable residential flooring — it's waterproof, scratch-resistant, and can last 50+ years. For wood look, LVP (luxury vinyl plank) is extremely durable, 100% waterproof, and costs far less than hardwood.

Is LVP better than laminate?

LVP is waterproof and can go in bathrooms, kitchens, and basements where laminate cannot. LVP typically costs $1–$3 more per sq ft but is more versatile. For dry areas only, quality laminate is a great budget alternative.

How much does it cost to floor a whole house?

Whole-home flooring for a 1,500–2,000 sq ft home typically costs $6,000–$20,000 depending on material. LVP throughout averages $9,000–$14,000. Solid hardwood throughout runs $15,000–$30,000+.
